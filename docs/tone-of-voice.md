# Dukanoh — Tone of Voice

## The North Star

**Insiders not consumers.**

Dukanoh speaks to the community as people who already get it. Never explain cultural terms. Never over-qualify. The audience knows what an Achkan is, what festive means, why a lehenga matters. Write like you're one of them.

---

## Brand Personality

**Exclusive. Culturally confident. Warm but straight-talking.**

- **Exclusive** — the resale mechanic is never the identity. Dukanoh is where rare, coveted pieces are discovered. Not where people offload old clothes.
- **Culturally confident** — South Asian terminology is used naturally, without asterisks or explanations.
- **Warm but straight-talking** — human and approachable, never corporate. Short sentences. No fluff.
- **Insider authority** — like Highsnobiety for South Asian fashion. You're speaking to people in the know.

---

## What Dukanoh Is Not

- Not a charity shop — never use "second life", "pre-loved", "going to a good home"
- Not a generic marketplace — never sound like eBay or Gumtree
- Not trying too hard — no forced hype, no exclamation marks unless truly warranted
- Not corporate — no "please be advised", no "kindly note", no passive voice

---

## Language Rules

**Use:**
- "pieces" not "items" or "products"
- "members" not "users" or "buyers/sellers"
- "discover" not "browse" or "shop"
- "find" not "purchase" or "buy" (in casual contexts)
- "list" as a verb ("list your pieces")
- Clean, confident statements — "Sold." not "This item has been sold."

**Avoid:**
- "second hand", "pre-owned", "pre-loved"
- "wardrobe clear out"
- "used"
- Explaining South Asian categories or garment types
- Filler words: "just", "simply", "easily"
- Exclamation marks in static or ambient copy

---

## Copy by Context

### Empty states
- No listings: `Nothing here yet — check back soon`
- No saved items: `Nothing saved yet. Start discovering.`
- No messages: `No messages yet`
- No search results: `Nothing matched that search`

### Onboarding & seller flow
- `List your pieces. Someone's been looking for them.`
- `Your next member is waiting.`
- `Verified sellers only. Apply to start selling.`

### Actions & labels
- Save: `Save`
- Sold listing badge: `Sold.`
- Verified seller: `Verified`
- Pro tier: `◆ Pro`

### Errors & warnings
Always warm, human, and give a clear next step. Never blame the member. Never be cold.

- Payment failed: `Something went wrong with your payment. Please try again.`
- Upload failed: `That didn't go through — give it another try.`
- Network error: `Can't connect right now. Check your connection and try again.`
- Account issue: `Something's not right with your account. Contact us and we'll sort it.`

### Pricing & transactions
- Prices are always bold. No preamble.
- "Sold." — full stop, one word. Confidence.
- "Buyer protection included." — factual, no hyperbole

### Promotional / marketing copy
- Lead with the piece, not the mechanic
- Cultural occasions (Eid, Diwali, Wedding season) are referenced naturally — no explanation
- Short. Let silence carry weight.

## Tone Examples

| Wrong | Right |
|---|---|
| "Browse our pre-loved collection" | "Discover pieces from the community" |
| "List your second-hand items" | "List your pieces" |
| "Users can buy and sell clothing" | "Members discover and sell with each other" |
| "Please be advised your order has been placed" | "Order confirmed." |
| "Easily sell your wardrobe clear out!" | "List your pieces. Someone's been looking for them." |
| "This beautiful lehenga (Indian bridal skirt)..." | "This lehenga..." |

## Audience

South Asian diaspora. UK-based. Fashion-conscious. Gen Z and millennial. They are insiders. Write accordingly.

---

## The Test

Before publishing any copy, ask:
1. Does it sound like something an insider would say?
2. Is there a shorter way to say it?
3. Does it explain anything that doesn't need explaining?
4. Would a corporate brand say this? (If yes — rewrite it.)
